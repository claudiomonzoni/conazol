What is Athlete’s Foot?

You don’t have to be an athlete to get it. Athlete’s foot is a fungal infection that affects everyone, especially men. It usually starts between the toes and can work its way to other parts of the foot. In some cases, both feet are infected.

Did you know? Athlete’s Foot Affects 10% of the World’s Population

What Causes it?

Athlete’s foot is caused by dermatophytes (fungi that affect the hair, skin, and nails). The fungus feeds on keratin, a protein found in these parts of the body, and so it thrives in the closed,warm, wet environments of the shoes and feet.

What are the Symptoms of Athlete’s Foot?

  • Itchy, scaly red rash between the toes.
  • Small, red blisters.
  • Dryness and scaly skin on the soles and sides of the foot.
  • Red ulcers that smell.

How Can I Prevent It?

  • Avoid going barefoot, especially in gyms and other public areas.
  • Wash and dry your feet thoroughly every day.
  • Stick to shoes that fit you properly, not tightly.
  • Wear socks that keep your feet dry.
